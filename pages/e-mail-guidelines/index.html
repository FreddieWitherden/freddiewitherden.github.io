General guidance

  • Plain-text only: HTML tends to add very little of meaningful value to an e-mail and more often than not is just a nuisance.
  • No confidentiality messages: as any good lawyer will tell you these are absolutely worthless and don't even come close to being legally binding. Please just don't bother! Should you need to contact me regarding a confidential issue please (snail-) mail me an non-disclosure agreement first.
  • Attachments: I am willing to accept most types of attachments with no size limit per-se—but as with everything: use your discretion. Please see below for more specific guidance.

My key can also be found on most key servers. In general I sign all outgoing mail with my PGP key but will only encrypt a message when either responding to an encrypted message or handling sensitive information.

Attachments

Broadly I divide attachments up into three categories:

The good
Open or ISO standardised formats such as plain-text, PNG, JPEG, OpenDocument and PDF.
The bad
Proprietary formats with good support from open-source software. Classic Microsoft Office formats such as .doc and .xls fall under this category.
The ugly
Proprietary formats with poor or non-existent support from open-source software. The new Office Open XML formats (.docx, .xlsx, …) fall in this category.

Although I have no objection to receiving attachments in the second or third categories I do not make any guarantees about being able to open/view them. Therefore, it is advisable that if you do send an attachment in one of these categories that you also attach it as either plain-text or PDF.
